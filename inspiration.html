Note

The people here are all YouTubers, as they are my main source of high-effort media. I generally don't watch actual movies and TV shows. For some reason, I used to be pretty sensitive to them. I still am, just not as much.

Alan Becker

Alan Becker is an animator and animation director on YouTube. His rise to fame began with a video titled Ani­mator vs. Ani­mation and ever since, he's been buil­ding con­tent off of animated stick figures play­ing around on his com­puter and in video games. I personally admire how he's taken such simple characters and given them such depth.

He isn't doing this alone, though. He now has a sizable animation team helping him out, as well as a few sound desi­gners and a com­poser helping him out. Many of the team's recent works can be described as cinematic mas­ter­pie­ces. If you'd like to check out his work, the link is here.

Kevin & Luke Lerdwichagul

This duo of animators-turned-showrunners started with a meme channel filled with what can generally be called high-effort crapposting (is there a better word for it?). They would then conso­lidate their efforts into a com­pany called Glitch Productions, where they began running more serious animated webseries. Today, they have suc­cess­fully ran 3 seasons of one of their own series, Meta Runner, and are currently backing another show, Murder Drones, written and directed by fellow animator Liam Vic­kers. I appreciate them giving other creators a creative out­let they would not have had otherwise. The video here is the pilot episode of Meta Runner.
